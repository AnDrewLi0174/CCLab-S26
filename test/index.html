[ Northern Lights ]

Scientific Name:

Aranea Borealis

Discovered by / at:

Private Andrew Li, upon HMS Aries, March 9th, 1866

Family:

The Aranea Family.Though the exact correlation is unclear.

Habitat:

An unknown recluse in the Atlantic Ocean.Some speculate that it originated from the mystery underwater kingdom Atlantis.

Appearance:

The Aranea Borealis isn't like any other spider you see. It resembles more of an atom than a spider, with its four tentacles moving in bizarre but efficient ways. It's known to glow vibrant colors in the dark, hence the name "Aranea Borealis", a wordplay on Aurora Borealis, the famous Northern Lights.

Key Characteristics:

  • Glows in the dark.
  • Preys on small fish but also potentially other animals if possible.
  • Flees from dangerous-looking objects like sharp rocks.
  • ...
